The Story

About Madhuri, the Elephant of Nandani

Madhuri — also called Mahadevi — spent over three decades in the care of the Nandani Jain math near Kolhapur, Maharashtra. Estimated to be 34–37 years old, she became a cherished figure in the community, participating in ceremonies and attracting devotees from across the region.

Veterinary assessments in 2023–2024 flagged serious concerns: chronic foot and joint conditions, open wounds, and stress responses linked to confinement and procession noise. Welfare reviewers recommended specialist care, enriched living conditions, and greatly reduced ceremonial duties.

In July 2025, the Bombay High Court ordered her transfer to Vantara — the Radhe Krishna Temple Elephant Welfare Trust in Jamnagar — to stabilise her health before any further decisions about her future home.

Madhuri remains at Vantara receiving specialist foot and joint treatment. HPC monitoring and court-reviewed compliance reports continue to guide any pathway toward a potential return to Kolhapur.

Legal & Welfare

Court Oversight & Key Findings

Judicial oversight has been continuous. Both the Bombay High Court and the Supreme Court of India have issued substantive directions that place elephant welfare at the centre of all future decisions regarding Madhuri.

Bombay High Court

Rehabilitation Order — 16 July 2025

The High Court upheld the HPC's recommendations in full, mandating specialist rehabilitation at Vantara and confirming that Madhuri's chronic medical needs could not be adequately addressed within her existing environment.

Supreme Court of India

Compliance & HPC Direction — Late July 2025

The Supreme Court ordered ongoing compliance reporting and full HPC involvement in crafting a welfare-first rehabilitation and potential return roadmap, with review continuing into 2026.

High Powered Committee

Welfare Assessment & Monitoring

The HPC's inspections in 2023–2024 formed the evidential basis for both court orders. The committee continues to monitor Madhuri's condition and provides periodic reports to the courts on rehabilitation progress.

Guiding Principle

Welfare Over Ceremony

Both courts affirmed that an elephant's right to health and humane treatment supersedes ceremonial or religious use when the two are in conflict — a precedent with implications across India's temple elephant community.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Why was Madhuri moved to Vantara?

Courts and the High Powered Committee cited chronic and progressive medical issues — including foot disease, joint conditions, and stress — that required specialist rehabilitation. Vantara in Jamnagar provides hydrotherapy, enriched enclosures, and the veterinary expertise needed to stabilise her health. The transfer was court-ordered and welfare-directed, not a permanent decision about her future home.

Can she return to Kolhapur?

A welfare-first roadmap is actively under discussion, including a potential Kolhapur-area satellite rehabilitation facility. Any return depends on verified medical benchmarks being met, a positive recommendation from the HPC, and judicial approval. There is no fixed timeline — medical and welfare standards must come first.

What is Vantara and why was it chosen?

Vantara — the Radhe Krishna Temple Elephant Welfare Trust in Jamnagar, Gujarat — is a specialist animal welfare facility with veterinary expertise in elephant care. Courts specifically directed the transfer there because of its capacity to provide the level of care Madhuri's medical condition requires, including hydrotherapy and enriched environments.

What is Madhuri's condition as of 2026?

Madhuri remains at Vantara receiving ongoing specialist treatment for her foot and joint conditions. Compliance reports submitted to the courts confirm continued veterinary management. Her situation is monitored by the HPC, and updates are reviewed by the courts on a rolling basis.
